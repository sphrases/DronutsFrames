What you need:

  • The Frame and two of the top plates
  • 8 little standoff-screw combos(3mm nylon/plastic is recommended; height has to be measured to fit the FC and receiver)
  • 4 nylon nuts and 4 nylon screws (also 3mm)
  • An emax Babyhawk or similar quadcopter
  • The 3d printed camera mount(I recommend tpu)

Preparation:
Check if every hole is big enough to fit the correct screw.
Use some light sandpaper on the edges of at least one top plate. (to protect the ESC wires)
You might want to insulate the bottom plate where you want the ESCs to mount with some electrical tape to prevent shorts between the carbon and the ESCs. Now grab a cool beverage and proceed.


Assembly:
Take the Babyhawk apart.
(It should be very straight forward, just don't rip anything apart)
Apply some new double sided sticky-tape to the underside of the FC (soft tape if you want it soft-mounted).

Lay the Babyhawk's intestines on the bottom plate, and screw down the motors.
Next insert the 4 screws in the bottom plate and screw down the 4 of the standoffs.
Carefully squeeze the first top plate (that you sanded down) between the fc and the bottom plate.
The ESCs should fit between the bottom plate and the center plate.

Check if everything is layed out correctly and stick down the FC with the tape you applied.
Now use some electrical tape to mount the ESCs (always use protection, carbon is conductive!)

Use the last 4 standoffs and mount the top plate together with the camera mount...
